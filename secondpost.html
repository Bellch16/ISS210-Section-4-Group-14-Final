Laws Involving Zoo Animals

A lack of laws protecting animals is a big part of why they are being mistreated.

Posted by Erin on April 28, 2023

There are many documented cases of animal abuse in private zoos, such as the zoo in the Netflix documentary “Tiger King''. These types of zoos are usually animal exhibits that have not been evaluated by the Association of Zoos and Aquariums (AZA).

Every state has its own animal cruelty laws but most of them don’t pertain to exhibited animals. America’s Animal Welfare Act attempts to protect exhibited animals, but it only applies to the majority of warm blooded mammals, and does not apply to any cold blooded animals like Reptiles. Many regulations act to protect a species as a whole, but not the individual animals.

One of the main issues concerning zoos is that the laws and enforcement protecting the animals is lacking. A big step we can take to improve animal welfare in zoos is to increase the amount of laws that protect animals, or to change and improve existing laws like the American Welfare Act. Only 10 percent of zoos in the US are accredited. Some of these laws only pertain to accredited zoos, so many animal exhibits that aren’t accredited ignore these laws. Zoos need to focus on science, conservation, and education, not making money.
