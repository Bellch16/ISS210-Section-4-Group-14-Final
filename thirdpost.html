The Problem With Zoos

By Austin Waller

Posted by on April 28, 2023

Economics Vs. Ethics

Zoos are meant to be places where people can come to learn about the animals of the world and a place where an animal can come after it is rescued from its original habitat. However today it is quite common to run into stories of physical and mental abuse against the animals that make them all of their money. That is why there needs to be a system of third party certification for all zoos to ensure that they have the resources to take care of the animals correctly and that they are treating the animals properly and according to the law.

Obviously it would make sense that zoos have to be economically conscious as they have large expenses every month such as land tax, electricity, water, and food for the animals. however too often zoos put their money before their animals. out of over 2,800 zoos in america less than 10 percent of them are AZA acreddited. AZA accreditation promises the highest standards for animal care and welfare. The way it should work is any non-acreddited zoo should not be allowed to posess animals for entertainment in the first place.

Why We Need Them

Not all zoos are bad, Earth is currently going through what is being called the “Sixth mass extinction” and by researching and breeding many threatened and endangered species zoos are a large helper in making sure that our wildlife does not go extinct and ensuring that we know how to keep their population rising in the wild. However the ethical treatment of the animals should always be the first priority of any zoo. Any zoo that is not able to pass a certification program should either not be able to possess the animals in the first place or should not be able to even call themselves a zoo. Zoos that do not take the treatment of their animals seriously need to be banned and we need to have a series of tests and checks to ensure that they are doing their job correctly and humanely.
