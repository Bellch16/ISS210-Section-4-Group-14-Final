About section

by Christian Bell, and Erin Muller

There needs to be higher standards for zoos, and the motive behind them should be about helping animals rather than focusing on their entertainment value.

We chose this topic because animal rights are very important, but zoo and exhibited animals usually get left out of the media, and law enforcement. As a pre-veterinary major, I have learned about, and experienced people lacking in taking care of their animals, whether it be dogs and cats or cows and horses. The same goes for animals in zoos, who are not well enough protected, and only have one law protecting them, the Animal Welfare Act. Animals' lives are at stake and it is important that more people are informed on how to protect them.

The oldest zoos were in Ancient Egypt, where animals were kept in cages to display the owners wealth. This shows that in history, zoos are used more for entertainment and for the animals' monetary value. While the oldest zoos were in Ancient Egypt the first modern zoo was created in the 18th century in Paris. This was during the Enlightenment and people wanted to learn/study more about animals so to keep them nearby they created zoos.

They were more like museums than natural habitats. This expands into today's version of zoos. More open areas, semi-natural habitats, and usually located in urban areas. Many zoos are used as entertainment rather than what their main purpose should be: education and conservation.
